Shrimp Tempura

Tempura batter frying is a method that dates back to mid-16th century Japan. This method of frying allows the food to stay extremely crispy while retaining less oil on the food. Making it healthier option than deep fried foods commonly found in the States. The most common way to eat tempura is with Ten-tsuyu sauce. This sauce made with fish stock, mirin and soy sauce. Although there are many types of tempura, shrimp tempura is definitely one of the most popular versions. It goes well with almost anything as you can eat it alone, with rice, soba noodles and many more.

Ingredients

  • 10 Prawns
  • Potato or Corn Starch
  • 3 Cups of Neutral Oil
  • 1 Large Egg
  • 3/4 Cup Iced Water
  • 1 Cup All Purpose Flour
  • 3/4 Cup of Dashi
  • 3 Tbsp. Soy Sauce
  • 2 Tbsp. Mirin
  • 2 Tsp. Sugar

Insutrctions

Dipping Sauce

  1. Gather the dashi, soy sauce, mirin and sugar. This will be used to create the Ten-tsuyu sauce.
  2. Combine all the ingredients in a pot and simmer on low until the sugar dissolves. Remove from heat and set aside.

Frying

  1. Score the shrimp in order to straighten it. Pat the shrimp dry to remove any moisture.
  2. In a wok or pot, heat up around 1-2 inches of oil to 350ÂºF and keep it at this temperature at all times. To check if the oil is ready, dip the tip of your chopsticks in the oil and if it bubbles, it is ready.
  3. While the oil is heating up, prepare the batter. Gather the egg, flour and iced water
  4. Sift the flour into a large bowl.
  5. Crack the cold egg and iced water into a measuring cup and mix. Discard the foam on the surface.
  6. Slowly pour the egg mixture into the batter and mix. Try not to overmix the batter as it is okay to leave some clumps. Keep the batter cold at all times, it is recommended to make the batter right before frying.
  7. Dust the shrimp with starch and dip it into the batter. Let the excess drip off the shrimp and place it gently into the frying oil.
  8. Deep fry the shrimp for about 2 minutes or until golden brown. Do not put too many in at once and do not overcook shrimp.
  9. When the shrimp is done frying, place aside on a rack or plate lined with paper towel to blot the excess oil off.
  10. Serve with dipping sauce and enjoy!
