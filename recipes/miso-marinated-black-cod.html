Miso Marinated Black Cod

Black Miso Cod, best known from the michelin star restaurant Nobu, is a flavorful yet light dish. Contrary to it's name Black Miso Cod is not actually cod. This Japanese dish is actually made with Sablefish fillets soaked in a marinated of Saikyo Miso, traditionally for up to 5-7 days. The marinade is called Saikyozuke, once the fish is cooked through grilling or broiling, it is called Saikyo Yaki. The result of this long marinating time and delicate cooking results in a very soft and flaky fish, jam packed full of umami.

This buttery fish pairs well with rice, steamed or pickled vegetables and even soba noodles. The sharpness of the Saikyo Miso counteracts with the fattiness of the Sablefish and is paired well with a dry white wine. I highly recommend trying this simple dish that shines through it's preparation a go, it will surely impress everyone at the dinner table.

Ingredients

  • 4 Fillets Sablefish
  • 2 Tsp Kosher Salt
  • 2 Tbsp Sake

    • Miso Marinade

  • 6 Tbsp Saikyo Miso
  • 3 Tbsp Mirin
  • 3 Tbsp Sake

Instructions

  1. Gather all the ingredients together. Try to pick out the freshest fish possible from the market as you will be marinating it for a couple days.

Marinating (2-3 days from serving)

  1. Sprinke salt over the fish and set aside for 30 minutes. This will help draw out the moisture in the fish and reduce the fishy odor.
  2. In a bowl, mix together the saikyo miso, mirin and sake.
  3. Pour the marinade into a flat bottom air tight container
  4. Pour 2 Tbsp of sake over the fish to wash off the salt and gently pat dry with a paper towel. Do not rinse the fish with running water
  5. Put the fish inside the container and completely cover it with the marinade. Take a spoon and cover the top of the fish with the marinade as well. Cover it and let it sit for 2 to 3 days.
  6. After the fish is done marinating, remove the fish and wipe the marinade off completely. Do not leave excess on the fish since it will burn when cooking.

Cooking

  1. Preheat the broiler for about 5 minutes. There are commonly three broiler settings, turn it on high for this recipe.
  2. Place the fish onto a baking sheet lined with foil and broil for 8-10 minutes until the top has browned a bit. Cooking time varies by the thickness of each fillet so be wary. You do not need to flip the fish

Serving

  1. Once the fish is finished cooking, serve with white rice or soba noodles. I like to plate the fish on a bed of steamed vegetables for nice contrast. You may garnish it with bean sprouts or sliced radish for presentation.
