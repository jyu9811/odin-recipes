Miso Soup

Miso soup is one of the most well known staple foods in Japanese cuisine. It is a soup made from miso, a fermented soy bean paste. There are three different types of miso: red, yellow and white. Each type of miso has a distinct flavor profile and can be used to bring out wonderful flavors in many different recipes. The core ingredients of miso soup are miso paste, dashi broth, tofu, seaweed, tofu and is commonly topped with scallions as well. This easily made soup can be a source of comfort for many.

Ingredients

  • 4 Cups of Water
  • 6 in. Piece of Kombu (rinsed)
  • 1 oz. Dried Kombu Flakes
  • 1 oz. Dried Seaweed (soaked in warm water)
  • 4 oz. Silken Tofu
  • 3 Tbsp. White Miso Paste
  • 1 Tbsp. Chopped Scallions

Instructions

  1. Put the kombu and water together in a pot and bring it to a boil on medium heat. Once you reach a rolling boil, remove the kombu and add the kombu flakes. Simmer on low for 5 minutes. Remove the foams on the surface for a clearer broth.
  2. Strain the broth through a sieve, do not press on the kombu flakes and discard them.
  3. Bring the dashi to a boil again and add the seaweed along with the tofu. Cook for 3 minutes.
  4. Turn off the heat and mix in the miso paste in the soup. Stir well with chopsticks until the miso has dissolved completely.
  5. Top with scallions and serve immediately
